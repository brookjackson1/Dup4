Famous F1 Circuits

Explore the most iconic racing circuits in Formula 1

Legendary Racing Venues

Formula 1 races on some of the world's most challenging and prestigious circuits. Each track has its own unique character and history.

Monaco Grand Prix

Location: Monte Carlo, Monaco

Circuit Length: 3.337 km (2.074 mi)

First Race: 1950

The most prestigious race in Formula 1, held on the narrow streets of Monte Carlo. Known for its tight corners, elevation changes, and the famous tunnel section.

Silverstone Circuit

Location: Silverstone, England

Circuit Length: 5.891 km (3.660 mi)

First Race: 1950

Home of British motorsport and host of the first-ever Formula 1 World Championship race. Famous for its high-speed corners like Copse, Maggots, and Becketts.

Monza Circuit

Location: Monza, Italy

Circuit Length: 5.793 km (3.600 mi)

First Race: 1950

The "Temple of Speed" and Ferrari's home race. One of the fastest circuits on the calendar with long straights and passionate Italian fans known as the Tifosi.

Spa-Francorchamps

Location: Stavelot, Belgium

Circuit Length: 7.004 km (4.352 mi)

First Race: 1950

One of the longest and most challenging circuits, famous for Eau Rouge corner complex. Known for unpredictable weather and dramatic elevation changes through the Ardennes forest.

Formula 1 History

Discover the rich heritage of Formula 1 racing since 1950

The Birth of Formula 1

Formula One (F1) was founded in 1950 as the pinnacle of international open-wheel motor racing. The FIA Formula One World Championship has been one of the premier forms of racing around the world since its inaugural season in 1950.

Key Milestones

  • 1950: First World Championship race at Silverstone, UK
  • 1958: Introduction of the Constructor's Championship
  • 1977: First turbocharged engine wins a race
  • 2014: Introduction of hybrid power units
  • 2022: Major regulation changes for closer racing

Legendary Eras

The Turbo Era (1977-1988)

Revolutionary turbocharged engines producing over 1,000 horsepower in qualifying trim.

The Hybrid Era (2014-Present)

Advanced hybrid power units combining internal combustion with electric motors for efficiency and performance.
